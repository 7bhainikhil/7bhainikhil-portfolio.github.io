Sentiment Analysis of Review

This research paper aims to provide an in-depth exploration of the use of machine learning techniques for sentiment analysis in the context of college reviews. Sentiment analysis, a sub-field of natural language processing, has gained significant attention due to the proliferation of user-generated content on the internet. Reviews play a crucial role in shaping consumer decisions and business strategies, making sentiment analysis a valuable tool for understanding public opinion. In this research, the sentiments of college going students are reviewed. By consolidating research in this field, this paper offers valuable insights for researchers and practitioners aiming to harness the power of sentiment analysis in the era of big data. The polarity score of each review is calculated using the VADER lexical method. The polarity score is used to categorise the reviews in positive, neutral, and negative domains. These categories are used as labels to the reviews for supervised machine learning techniques compared in this paper. In addition, review statements are used to generate the input features from the corpus of words formed by reviews. The paper presents a comparison of the results obtained by applying various machine learning algorithms.
